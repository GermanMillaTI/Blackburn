WHY IS THIS STUDY BEING DONE?

 

The purpose of this Study is to collect photos, videos and audios of individuals’ head, face and hands in still and moving poses, under various lighting conditions inside of a photogrammetry light stage. The data collected in this Study may be used to develop and improve products and services, which could include technologies and algorithms that operate on an iPhone and other related products and for the purposes set forth in this Consent.

 

WHAT WILL I BE ASKED TO DO?

 

You will be invited to 1 or more study sessions to complete the following capture sequences. A member of the Sponsor’s team conducting the Study (“Study Team”) will walk you through the all or part of the Study procedures listed below that are specific to the study session.

 

IPD Measurements (5 minutes)

  • You will be seated at a table with the moderator seating across from you.
  • You will be instructed on how to hold and position the pupillometer device (a device for measuring the distance between the right and left eye’s pupil).
  • While you are looking through the pupillometer, the moderator will look through the opposite end of the pupillometer, make adjustments and take record IPD measurements.

 

Face Capture Sequence (10 minutes)

  • You will be seated inside of a dynamic lighting rig and instructed to perform 6 to 10 head movements while being recorded with a recording device. Head movements involve turning your head side to side, and tilting up and down, and rotating it in a circular pattern. Each head movement will take 30 seconds.
  • Optionally, you may be asked to wear study-provided or self-provided glasses, headwear, and earbuds / headphones in one or more capture sequences.

 

Head Capture Sequence (normal clothing, no wig cap) (20 minutes)

  • You will be seated inside of a photogrammetry light stage and instructed to perform 10 to 15 facial expressions. For each expression, multiple cameras are capturing images of your head and face with dynamic lighting conditions. Each image capture for the facial expressions will take 3 seconds.
  • You will be instructed to read a short paragraph of words (pangram), available in different languages, while audio and video of you are being recorded. There are 3-5 of these capture sequences and each will take 10 seconds.

 

Head Capture Sequence (wide collar T-shirt and wig cap) (40 minutes)

  • You will be provided a wide collar T-shirt and a wig cap and access to a change room to prepare for the next capture sequence.
  • You will be seated inside of a photogrammetry light stage and instructed to perform 25 to 30 facial expressions. For each expression, multiple cameras are capturing images of your head and face with dynamic lighting conditions. Each image capture for the facial expressions will take 3 seconds.
  • You will be instructed to read a short paragraph of words (pangram), available in different languages, while audio and video of you are being recorded. There are 3-5 of these capture sequences and each will take 10 seconds.

 

Hands Capture Sequence (wide collar T-shirt and wig cap) (15 minutes)

  • You will be instructed to hold your hand in front of your face for a static pose photo capture, and 2 to 4 poses and photos will be taken for each hand with slight variation in position and fingers gesture.

 

The Study team will use the video and photo images to generate a photo-realistic 3D model of your face and head that will be considered part of the Study Data (see below).

WHO CAN PARTICIPATE IN THIS STUDY?

 

Before starting this Study, you must meet all the following criteria:

  • Be willing and able to participate in the Study procedures described in this Consent.

  • Are currently taking photosensitizing medications or have any known photosensitizing medical conditions.

WHAT DATA WILL BE COLLECTED AND HOW LONG WILL IT BE KEPT?

 

As part of this Study, the following data may be collected or accessed (collectively, “Study Data”):

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, Apple ID. This information will be collected during Study recruitment. Your Contact Information will be deleted within five years after the Study ends.
  • Demographic Data: Age, gender, ethnicity, height, weight, hair color, hair texture, eye color, skin tone. This information, some of which may be sensitive personal information, will be collected by the Study Team during Study onboarding in order to assess the impact of this Demographic Data on the design, development, and/or use of the Study device. Your Coded Demographic Data may be retained as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Consent.
  • Photo/Video/Audio: Photos and videos of your head, face and hands, and speech audio data; in addition, the Study team will use the video and photo images to generate a photo-realistic 3D model of your face and head that will be considered part of the Study Data and your Photo/Video/Audio data. Your Coded Photo/Video/Audio, including the 3D model, will be deleted within 5 years after the Study ends.
  • Biometric Information: As stated above, we will collect video and photo images of your face, hands and body and audio recordings of your voice, and generate a 3D model of your face and head. It is not our intent to use this information to identify you in any way, but it may be used to develop algorithms that could be used to identify individuals. Some laws may consider these images to be “biometric data” and sensitive personal information and require your explicit consent for collection, which you provide when you sign or otherwise indicate your acceptance of this Consent. Your Biometric Information will be deleted within 5 years after the date listed on this Consent.

 

HOW WILL MY STUDY DATA BE KEPT CONFIDENTIAL?

 

Your Contact Information will be held securely by the Study Organizer, and access limited to those on the Study Team who need access to manage the Study. Your remaining Study Data will be separated from your Contact Information and assigned a code (we use the word “Coded” to describe these data that is, “Coded Study Data”). Your Coded Study Data will be maintained by the Study Team directly in a central study database with other subjects’ Coded Study Data.[AS1][PC2]

 

All Study Data that identifies you will be protected and treated as confidential. However, total confidentiality cannot be guaranteed. It is possible that members of the Study Team could identify you from your Coded Study Data. For example, if the Study Data includes photo images of you, there could be unauthorized access to the database used to store your Study Data. Further, if you are in multiple studies, your Study Data may be combined across those studies, which could increase the risk that someone could identify you. There may be other privacy risks that we cannot anticipate now.

 

For more information on how Study Data will be kept secure and confidential, please contact the Study Lead listed above.
