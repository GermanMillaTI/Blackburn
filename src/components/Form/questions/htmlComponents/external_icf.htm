Study Lead: Pei-Ta Chu, peita@apple.com

 

You are receiving this Study Informed Consent Form (“Consent”) because you were asked to participate in a voluntary research Study run by TELUS International, AI (“Study Organizer”) on behalf of Apple Inc. (the “Study Sponsor”).

WHY IS THIS STUDY BEING DONE?

 

This study is being done to aid in the development and improvement of software products (for example, FaceID and Memoji).  

 

WHAT WILL HAPPEN IF I TAKE PART IN THIS STUDY?

 

You will receive Study information from a TELUS International Employee working with Study Sponsor and undertaking study recruiting and enrollment (“Study Enrollment Vendor”). The Study Enrollment Vendor will provide you with this Consent form, which, if you elect to participate, you will sign electronically. You will receive a specific date and time for your session from the Study Enrollment Vendor and instructions on getting to the study site, and information on the health screening procedures (for example, taking your temperature) we will undertake prior to admitting you to the study site for your study session.

A member of the Study Enrollment Vendor will check you in at the Study Site.  Then, a member of Study Sponsor’s team conducting the Study (“Study Team”) will walk you through the Study procedures listed below.

 

Ear Mold (15 minutes)

Impression of the outer ear

  • A trained audiologist will be using a soft silicone molding compound to take an impression of the entrance to the ear canal and the outer ear.
  • Your ear canal will be protected with a cotton ball.
  • During the molding process, you will be sitting while the silicone compound is placed onto your ear.  The compound sets in approximately two minutes.  The ear mold will be removed from your outer ear after approximately three more minutes.  This will be done to both left and right ears.
  • There is a slight possibility that if the inspection of the mold quality finds some issues in the mold, the impression would be repeated.

WHAT DATA WILL BE COLLECTED AND HOW LONG WILL IT BE KEPT?

 

As part of this Study, the following information may be collected or accessed about you or related to your participation as part of the Study (collectively, we call this “Study Data”):

  • Contact Information: name, email address.  Your contact information (other than your signed consent form) will be deleted within one year after the Study ends.
  • Demographic Data: Birth year, gender, and ethnicity. This information will be collected by the Study Team during Study onboarding. Your Coded Demographic Data will be deleted within 10 years after the Study ends.
  • Documented Study Data: Height, weight, hair color, hair length, hair quality, facial hair, eye color.  Your Coded Documented Study Data will be deleted within 10 years after the Study ends.
  • Photo/Video/Image Data: Scanned 3D impression of the outer ear, and audio recording of sound from your ears.  While we do not intend to use these images to identify you or develop algorithms that identify you, some laws may consider these images to be “biometric identifiers,” so we disclose this information for your knowledge.  Your Coded Image Data will be deleted within 10 years after the Study ends.

 

To request deletion of your Study Data, and to see other rights you may have with respect to your data, see “What Data Rights Do I Have?” section below.

 

HOW WILL STUDY DATA RELATED TO ME BE KEPT CONFIDENTIAL?

 

Your Contact Information will be held securely by the Study Enrollment Vendor and access limited to its personnel who need access to administer and conduct the Study.  The Study Enrollment Vendor will provide the Study Team with a unique code assigned to you and this code will be associated with the Study Data collected through the Study (we use the word “Coded” to describe this data for example “Coded Study Data”). Your Coded Study Data will be maintained by Study Sponsor and the Study Enrollment Vendor will maintain your Contact Information and your signed Consent form

 

Although all Study Data that identifies you will be protected and treated as confidential, total confidentiality cannot be guaranteed. Further, members of the Study Team and Study Sponsor may be able to identify you just from your Coded Study Data (for instance if the Study Data includes photo or scanned images of you), or there could be unauthorized access to, or security breaches of, the systems used to store your Study Data. If you are in multiple studies, your Study Data may be combined and there is a risk that you may be identifiable. There may be other privacy risks that we have not foreseen.

 

For more information on how Study Data will be kept secure and confidential, please contact the Study Lead listed above.
